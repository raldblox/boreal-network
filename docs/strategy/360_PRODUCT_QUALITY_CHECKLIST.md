# Boreal 360 Product Quality Checklist

This checklist defines the quality bar Boreal should hold across brand, product, supply, trust, and execution.

It is for decision-making, not just polish review.
A feature that looks impressive but weakens trust, proof, or focus should fail this checklist.

## How To Use This Checklist

Use it when:

- shaping the first wedge
- reviewing roadmap priorities
- assessing whether a launch surface is ready
- checking whether messaging and product still match each other
- deciding whether a new feature helps Boreal become more complete or merely more complex

Score each area:

- `0` missing
- `1` weak or unclear
- `2` acceptable
- `3` strong
- `4` best-in-class for current stage

## 1. Category Clarity

- Can a smart outsider understand what Boreal does in one sentence?
- Does the product read as a better path from request to completion, not as architecture looking for a problem?
- Is the first wedge obvious?
- Is the category legible without needing ontology vocabulary?
- Do messaging and product both reinforce the same core job?

Failure signs:
- people call it a chatbot, PM tool, or generic marketplace
- explanation requires too much theory before pain is clear

## 2. Buyer Problem Clarity

- Is the buyer pain specific and felt now?
- Does the product clearly solve dropped context, fake completion, weak ownership, or missing proof?
- Is the target user already motivated to pay?
- Is the request class high-importance enough to justify a stronger workflow?

Failure signs:
- the problem sounds interesting but not urgent
- the buyer could just use a doc, chat thread, or existing vendor flow with no real loss

## 3. ICP And Wedge Discipline

- Is the first buyer clearly defined?
- Are the first request classes explicit?
- Are excluded users and anti-personas named?
- Are roadmap decisions being judged against the wedge instead of broad future fantasies?

Failure signs:
- new features keep drifting toward `everyone`
- supply-side expansion outruns demand-side proof

## 4. Request Intake Quality

- Can the buyer bring a messy ask without being punished for not writing a perfect brief?
- Does the intake flow clarify goals, constraints, proof requirements, and what AI already tried?
- Does Boreal reveal ambiguity instead of hiding it?
- Is there a clear path from intake to next action?

Failure signs:
- users need to know the full scope before Boreal becomes useful
- messy high-value requests feel awkward to submit

## 5. Routing And Ownership Quality

- Is it always clear who owns the next step?
- Does the system route to the right lead before exploding into sub-tasks?
- Can users tell the difference between buyer, lead, specialist, reviewer, and agent roles?
- Does the product minimize dropped handoffs?

Failure signs:
- multiple actors appear active but nobody feels accountable
- task trees appear before a real lead path is clear

## 6. Fulfillment Continuity Quality

- Does the original request stay attached through clarification, commitment, execution, proof, and closeout?
- Can users reconstruct what happened without hunting across tools?
- Does Boreal feel like one work thread rather than a stitched dashboard?

Failure signs:
- artifacts, discussions, and payout state drift into separate worlds
- the user has to restate context repeatedly

## 7. Proof And Verification Quality

- Is proof part of the workflow, not an afterthought?
- Are required artifacts, reviews, and acceptance criteria visible?
- Can the system distinguish output from verified completion?
- Are revision and review flows explicit?

Failure signs:
- `done` means a message was generated, not a result was accepted
- screenshots or artifacts are missing or hard to inspect

## 8. Commerce And Payout Quality

- Are price, commitment, and payout boundaries clear?
- Can users tell what they are agreeing to?
- Is closeout tied to proof and acceptance state?
- Are revisions and disputes modeled honestly enough for current stage?

Failure signs:
- commercial state lives only in chat
- payout logic is implied but not legible

## 9. Trust And Safety Quality

- Are identity, permissions, and authority legible?
- Can buyers tell what a human owns versus what an agent or runtime is doing?
- Are risk boundaries visible when work becomes sensitive?
- Does the system avoid overstating automation confidence?

Failure signs:
- ambiguous actor responsibility
- product language implies full trust where only partial trust exists

## 10. Human Plus AI Boundary Quality

- Does the product clearly show where AI helps and where human judgment still matters?
- Does it prevent AI-only false closure on serious requests?
- Can human intervention be inserted without breaking the thread?
- Are local runtime or verification-sensitive steps treated honestly?

Failure signs:
- humans appear only as fallback decoration
- AI output is treated as completion by default

## 11. Supply Quality

- Is the early supply bar high enough to produce repeatable proof?
- Are suppliers evaluated on communication, execution, and proof discipline?
- Can Boreal match for quality, not just availability?
- Is curated supply treated as a strategic advantage rather than an embarrassment?

Failure signs:
- supply volume grows faster than completion quality
- inconsistent closeout makes the market feel unreliable

## 12. UX Clarity

- Is the current state obvious?
- Is the next action obvious?
- Is the latest proof obvious?
- Are request, commitment, fulfillment, and closeout states understandable without training?
- Does the interface reduce anxiety instead of increasing it?

Failure signs:
- important status is buried
- users cannot tell what changed or what is blocking completion

## 13. Speed And Responsiveness

- Does the product feel fast enough for urgent work?
- Can the buyer move from messy ask to a meaningful next step quickly?
- Are review and proof loops efficient?
- Does the product reduce coordination drag rather than add ceremony?

Failure signs:
- flow feels slower than current patchwork
- the user must do too much structuring before value appears

## 14. Repeatability And Memory

- Does successful completion create advantage for the next request?
- Is prior context reusable?
- Can reputation emerge from actual completed work?
- Does the system improve with repeated use for the same buyer or supply lane?

Failure signs:
- every request feels like starting from zero
- no compounding trust or routing advantage appears

## 15. Brand And Narrative Quality

- Does Boreal sound serious, clear, and direct?
- Does public messaging stay close to product truth?
- Are hero claims anchored in a workflow users can imagine?
- Is the brand anti-fake-completion without sounding anti-AI?

Failure signs:
- copy sounds smarter than the product feels
- brand voice outruns proof

## 16. Launch Readiness Quality

- Can the product support one real paid request end to end?
- Are current claims honest under `live vs target` rules?
- Is the CTA aligned with the actual operating motion?
- Are the best screenshots, demos, or walkthroughs available?
- Can a narrow pilot succeed without manual chaos?

Failure signs:
- marketing promises open scale but ops are still curated
- team cannot confidently run a real pilot repeatedly

## 17. Metrics Quality

- Are you measuring funded requests, completion quality, proof acceptance, repeat usage, and closeout speed?
- Are metrics tied to the wedge instead of vanity growth?
- Can the team tell whether Boreal is actually reducing fragmentation and false completion?

Failure signs:
- success is defined by signups or impressions only
- no clear link between metrics and durable market fit

## 18. Research And Market Truth Quality

- Are product decisions supported by observed buyer pain, not just internal logic?
- Are external references used to validate the problem and timing rather than force a grand narrative?
- Is the team collecting evidence from real request pilots?

Failure signs:
- the story feels correct but is not grounded in user behavior
- research is cited as decoration rather than decision support

## 19. Expansion Discipline

- Does each expansion preserve the core request-to-completion truth?
- Are new user types additive to the wedge instead of diluting it?
- Does the team know what must stay true even as Boreal expands into broader network behavior?

Failure signs:
- expansion changes the product's center of gravity
- adjacent stories overpower the original proof loop

## 20. Best-In-Class Question

At any moment, ask:

`If the buyer brought one serious request today, would Boreal feel like the most trustworthy, accountable, and complete path from ask to done for this stage of the company?`

If the answer is no, the roadmap should bias toward the weakest quality dimension above.

## Minimum Launch Bar For The First Wedge

Boreal should not feel launch-ready for the first wedge unless it is at least acceptable or stronger in these areas:

- category clarity
- buyer problem clarity
- request intake
- routing and ownership
- fulfillment continuity
- proof and verification
- commerce and payout clarity
- trust boundaries
- supply quality
- launch honesty

## Simple Rule

Boreal wins when it makes serious work feel more finishable.
Anything that weakens finishability, proof, or trust is not a quality improvement, even if it increases feature count.
