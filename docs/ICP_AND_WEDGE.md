# ICP And Wedge

This file defines who Boreal should serve first and where the first durable market wedge exists.

## Primary ICP

The first ICP is:

- startups and SMBs
- buying external digital work
- with urgent, high-importance requests
- where scope is partly ambiguous at intake
- and where execution may require both human judgment and AI-native capability

Reference signal:

- [Upwork's Spring 2026 SMB research](https://investors.upwork.com/news-releases/news-release-details/upwork-updates-spring-2026-ai-powered-innovations-help-small) says nearly half of SMBs report finding the right expertise as a top concern.

## Primary Buyer

The first buyer is usually:

- founder
- operator
- product lead
- growth lead
- technical lead

These buyers care about speed, accountability, and quality more than about browsing endless supply catalogs.

## First Wedge

The first wedge is:

AI-native digital service work for startups and SMBs where intake is fuzzy and fulfillment must stay coordinated.

Representative request classes:

- AI implementation and automation work
- research and analysis work
- MVP, specification, and prototype work
- media and content production work
- specialist operator work that needs review, proof, and payout
- public problem requests where accepted work can become a reusable solution
- reusable service plans where inspection is public but reruns consume inference or execution credits

## Current Launch Motion

The best first motion for this wedge is:

- paid buyer requests first
- curated supply whitelist second
- optional request grants where multiple people want the same problem solved

That keeps validation focused on real demand and real fulfillment pressure.

The whitelist should prefer:

- real scenarios where AI-only plans break
- workflows that still need human judgment or verification
- supply that can explain what completed work should look like

These classes fit Boreal because they share the same properties:

- high urgency
- digital delivery
- repeatable demand
- mixed human and AI execution
- non-trivial scoping
- need for accountability after the first message
- potential for accepted artifacts to become reusable public solutions
- natural credit-metered reuse when a solution is run again with inference, provider APIs, workflow execution, or review

## What Not To Wedge On First

Do not anchor the first wedge on:

- commodity microtasks
- local physical services
- pure internal employee workflows
- fully bespoke consulting engagements that require heavy manual sales and delivery
- protocol tooling sold to developers before buyer demand exists

Those segments either compress margins, slow the feedback loop, or blur the product category too early.

## Why This Wedge Is Attractive

This wedge is attractive because:

- buyers already spend here
- AI is increasing both urgency and confusion
- the work is digital and therefore easier to verify and route
- buyers often need continuity after matching, not just discovery
- successful execution creates repeat demand and retained context

## Expansion Path

After the first wedge, Boreal can expand into:

1. repeat buyer workflows with curated supply
2. collective and team-based fulfillment
3. public solution surfaces funded by optional request grants
4. private supply and enterprise operator lanes
5. runtime-connected and peer-connected execution surfaces

Expansion should preserve the same root truth:

- external demand becomes one durable request thread

## Wedge Summary

The right first market is not "all work."

It is:

high-value digital work requests from startup and SMB buyers who need better demand capture, routing, execution continuity, and proof across human and AI supply.
