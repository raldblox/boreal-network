# Pitch Facts

This file is the pitch-safe fact sheet for Boreal Network.

Use it as the shortest trusted source for pitch decks, idea roasts, investor applications, and external summaries.

## Category

- Boreal category: `request-native work commerce`
- Boreal system shape: `request-native work network`
- Boreal interface reading: `chat-native`
- Boreal thesis reading: `intent-to-fulfillment for work`

## Core Definition

- Boreal turns a work request into one managed fulfillment thread.
- The same `Request` carries demand, routing, commitment, funding, fulfillment, proof, and payout.
- Supply can come from humans, agents, tools, providers, or runtimes.

## Problem

- external work demand often starts ambiguous
- static catalogs are weak when scoping is fuzzy
- chat alone loses accountability after intake
- isolated AI tools do not solve commitment, proof, payment, and payout

## Product Claim

- Boreal is the fulfillment thread for AI-native external work
- it keeps matching, execution, proof, and payment on one durable object

## Primary Buyer

- startups and SMBs
- founder-led and operator-led teams
- buying high-value digital work from outside supply

## First Wedge

- AI-native digital service work where intake is fuzzy and fulfillment needs continuity

Representative classes:

- AI implementation and automation
- research and analysis
- MVP and spec work
- media and content production
- specialist operator work

## Why Now

- AI-related external work is already growing fast on major marketplaces
- marketplaces are moving from transactions toward workflow and fulfillment infrastructure
- buyers are tired of isolated AI point tools
- enterprises and SMBs both want unified systems that help work move from idea to execution

## Revenue Model

- primary: take rate on funded request GMV
- secondary: premium routing, curation, and urgency
- later: private supply, enterprise software, payout or verification infrastructure

## Strategic Position

- Boreal is not a direct frontier-model competitor
- Boreal is not a generic freelancer marketplace clone
- Boreal is not an internal employee copilot suite
- Boreal is not an agency-first services business

## Why Boreal Can Win

- request-rooted instead of listing-rooted
- continuity through fulfillment instead of stopping at matching
- mixed human and AI supply from the start
- payment, proof, and payout modeled as first-class business truth

## Evidence Signals

- [Upwork](https://investors.upwork.com/news-releases/news-release-details/upwork-reports-fourth-quarter-and-full-year-2025-financial) reported AI-related work above `$300 million` annualized in Q4 2025 and AI integration and automation work growth above `90%` year over year
- [Fiverr](https://www.fiverr.com/news/fiverr-q1-2026-earnings) says it is moving from a transactional marketplace toward a sophisticated work platform
- [OpenAI](https://openai.com/index/next-phase-of-enterprise-ai/) says companies are tired of AI point solutions that do not talk to each other
- [Microsoft](https://blogs.microsoft.com/blog/2026/03/09/introducing-the-first-frontier-suite-built-on-intelligence-trust/) says customers do not want multiple AI tools stitched together

## What Must Not Be Claimed

Do not claim that Boreal today is:

- a winner-take-all global labor marketplace
- a general-purpose enterprise agent suite
- a protocol business first
- fully proven on every vertical

The correct claim is sharper:

Boreal is building the request-native work-commerce layer for external work that now needs mixed human and AI fulfillment.
